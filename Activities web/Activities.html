Projects

Lithium-Ion Battery

Lithium-Ion Battery

The lithium-ion battery is a rechargeable battery but overheating, short lifetime and under-performance in extreme temperature limit its comprehensive applications. In this project, we have designed a jacket containing Nanofluid. This Nanofluid has a high cooling tendency, lowering the battery's temperature. Thus, heightening its operating range and efficiency.

Stable Salt Reactor

Stable Salt Reactor

A fast reactor with no moderator, allowing it to destroy higher actinides such as plutonium, americium and curium, which inevitably accumulate in the reactor. Its rectangular core refueling is very simple and enables it to run virtually indefinitely without shutting down, improving its economic efficiency and reducing the stress on components arising from thermal cycling.

Biofuel from Biowaste

Biofuel from Biowaste

The aim of this project is to extract the majority of energy from the waste food and turn it into fuel and electricity. With processes like hydrothermal Liquefaction and anaerobic digestion, we extract crude bio-oil which refines into biofuel. Using biowaste, not only reduces pollution but also reduces carbon footprints.

Water Purification using TiO2

Water Purification using TiO2

It is a revolutionary technique of Water Purification. This project evolves a multi-step efficient solution for ultra-purification of industrial water using TiO2 powder, Silica gel and Rice Husk membranes. Hazardous hydrocarbons and Nano pollutants such as lead and chromium are being phased out making water safe for drinking.

The Ocean Cleanup

The Ocean Cleanup

It is a boat that moves like a snake and is operated remotely, traveling freely through the environment and cleaning the oceans. Using cellulose sheets packed with polysulphide polymer made of refining and petroleum by-products. As the polymer is hydrophobic by nature. If the polymer has absorbed the oil, it will turn into a sponge, and the oil will be squeezed out using rollers, allowing the sheets to be reused.

Membrane Technology

Membrane Technology

Membrane separation techniques have emerged as the most energy-efficient separation methods and have proven to be extremely useful as an alternative to traditional systems. Even though membrane processes are innovative, fouling is the biggest roadblock in its way. With the help of this project, we can resolve this problem and make the wastewater reusable for agriculture, industrial and domestic purposes.
